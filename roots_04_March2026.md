## Irene Ramos, our Research Fellow recommends:

### Caring for Science, Society and Self
Sellberg, M. M., Cockburn, J., Holden, P. B., & Lam, D. P. M. (2021). Towards a caring transdisciplinary research practice: navigating science, society and self. Ecosystems and People, 17(1), 292–305. https://doi.org/10.1080/26395916.2021.1931452

As a researcher in open science and sustainability, I often work with communities navigating the tensions between traditional academic systems and collaborative practices. I return to this paper for inspiration on a way of doing research that is rigorous, relevant, and that supports an ethics of care in academia beyond self-care.

The authors synthesize reflections on the challenges and possibilities of conducting transdisciplinary research in sustainability, and develop a theory of change that envisions a system where science, society and self are equally valued. They outline pathways for systemic change, including recognition of a wider range of outputs, flexible funding, infrastructure that supports collaboration, and changes in training and mentorship. Sound familiar? As I read, I kept mentally swapping “transdisciplinary” for “open”, noting how closely these visions for research align, even if conversations unfold in parallel.

The authors suggest organizations bridging research, policy, and practice (like OLS!) can enable environments for new practices to flourish, which I think includes connecting communities working toward shared goals.

Read the full paper here: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/26395916.2021.1931452
